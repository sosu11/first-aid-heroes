Cuts & Scrapes

The skin gets broken and it might bleed a little.
👉 What to do
  1. Wash your hands first if you can.
  2. Rinse the cut gently with clean water to wash out any dirt.
  3. Press on it with a clean cloth or tissue until the bleeding slows down.
  4. Cover it with a bandage to keep it clean.
🛑 Get a grown-up if…

the cut is deep or big, it won't stop bleeding, it was made by something dirty or rusty, or something is stuck inside it.

Cool fact: Your body makes a scab like a tiny shield to protect the cut while it heals. Try not to pick it!
💙

Bruises & Minor Sprains

A bump breaks tiny blood vessels (a bruise), or a joint gets twisted (a sprain).
👉 What to do — remember R.I.C.E.
  1. Rest — stop and take a break from using it.
  2. Ice — hold a cold pack (or ice in a cloth) on it for about 20 minutes. Never put ice straight on bare skin.
  3. Compress — a grown-up can wrap it snugly with a bandage.
  4. Elevate — prop it up on a pillow, higher than your heart.
🛑 Get a grown-up if…

it hurts a lot, it's very swollen, it looks bent or crooked, or you can't move it or walk on it.

Cool fact: A bruise changes color as it heals — purple, then greenish, then yellow. That's your body cleaning up!
👃

Nosebleeds

A tiny blood vessel inside the nose pops. It looks like a lot, but it's usually not serious.
👉 What to do
  1. Sit up and lean forward a little. Don't tip your head back!
  2. Pinch the soft part of your nose shut.
  3. Breathe through your mouth and hold for 10 minutes — no peeking!
  4. Gently let go. Try not to blow or pick your nose after.
🛑 Get a grown-up if…

it doesn't stop after 10–15 minutes, it started after a hard bump to the head, or there's a whole lot of blood.

Why lean forward? Leaning back sends blood down your throat and can make your tummy feel yucky. Forward is best.
🔥

Minor Burns & Sunburn

Something hot — or too much sun — hurts the skin. It may turn red and sting.
👉 What to do
  1. Get away from whatever is hot, right away.
  2. Cool it under cool (not icy) running water for about 20 minutes.
  3. Do not put ice, butter, toothpaste, or creams on a fresh burn.
  4. Cover it loosely with a clean cloth.
For sunburn: get into the shade, sip water, and cool the skin. Aloe vera helps it feel better. Best trick of all: sunscreen and a hat stop sunburn before it starts! ☀️
🛑 Get a grown-up if…

the burn blisters, is bigger than your hand, is on the face, hands, or private areas, or came from fire, chemicals, or electricity.

Choking

Food or an object gets stuck and blocks someone's breathing. This one is serious — act fast.
👀 First, look — can they cough, talk, or make noise?
YES 😮‍💨They're getting some air. Stay with them and tell them to keep coughing hard.
NO 🚨They can't cough, talk, or breathe (or they grab their throat). This is an emergency!
👉 If they can't breathe — get help & act
  1. Yell for a grown-up and have someone call 911 right now.
  2. If you've been taught how: give 5 back blows between the shoulder blades with the heel of your hand.
  3. Then give 5 belly thrusts — hands just above the belly button, pull in and up.
  4. Keep switching — 5 back blows, 5 belly thrusts — until the object pops out or help arrives.
🛑 The #1 rule

Always get a grown-up immediately — choking is a real emergency. The most important thing YOU can do is get an adult fast and call 911. Belly thrusts are for kids and grown-ups, never for babies — a grown-up must help a choking baby.

Temperature & Fevers

A fever is when your body gets warmer than normal (normal is about 98.6°F / 37°C).
👉 What to do
  1. Tell a grown-up how you feel.
  2. Rest and take it easy — your body is busy fighting germs.
  3. Sip lots of water and cool drinks.
  4. Wear light, comfy clothes — don't bundle up too much.
🛑 Get a grown-up if…

you feel really sick, the fever is very high, you're hard to wake up, you have trouble breathing, or a bad headache or stiff neck. A grown-up decides about any medicine — never take it on your own.

Cool fact: A fever is your body turning up the heat to help win the battle against germs. A thermometer measures how warm you are.

⭐ ALWAYS get a grown-up right away if you see…

  • Someone who can't breathe, is choking, or won't wake up
  • Bleeding that won't stop
  • A burn from fire, chemicals, or electricity — or a big burn
  • A hard hit to the head, or a bone that looks bent or broken
  • Anyone who seems very sick or very scared

When it's big, call 911. You are never in trouble for getting help.

This guide teaches first aid basics so you can be a great helper. It does not replace a grown-up, a nurse, or a doctor. In a real emergency, always get an adult and call 911 (or your local emergency number).
